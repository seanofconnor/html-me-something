Collective Nouns for Animals

Many animals, mostly those that have been domesticated or of interest to hunters, have been assigned collective nouns - a name for a group of them. The Book of Saint Albans, published in 1486 details many odd words hunters would use.

Here are some of those names!

A group of boar are a herd.

A group of heron are a flight.

A group of raccoons are a gaze.

A group of squirrels are a scurry.

A group of skunks are a surfeit.

A group of ravens are an unkindness.

A group of bats are a cloud.

A group of larks are an exaltation.

Fun Fact! James Lipton of Inside the Actors Studio wrote a book called "An Exaltation of Larks" describing the collective nouns used by 15th century aristocrats.
